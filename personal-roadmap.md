# Personal roadmap — Pretext Lab

> **Private. Do not commit.** Add `notes/personal-roadmap.md` to `.gitignore`.

The public PRD describes the project as a research artifact. This file is the parallel doc that captures **why I'm actually building it** and how it ties to the job search. Kept separate so the public repo stays content-first, not portfolio-first.

---

## What this project is for

The Pretext lab is the centerpiece of my international job-search positioning for 2026. It exists because:

1. CVs and LinkedIn "About" sections don't differentiate Senior frontend engineers. Everyone has React + TypeScript + Next.js + design systems.
2. A piece of work that demonstrates **methodology** (not just shipping) is rare and self-selects for the kind of company I want — product-tier, AI-adjacent, remote-first, where engineering rigor is valued over framework familiarity.
3. The Pretext space is small enough that quality work gets noticed by the right people (Cheng Lou's network = ex-Facebook React / Midjourney / dev-tooling community).
4. The findings naturally generate LinkedIn content that isn't generic advice — every post is anchored in a specific number from a specific experiment.

If I get one inbound conversation from a hiring manager who read the site, the project paid for itself.

---

## Hiring outcomes (what success actually means)

Public PRD section 6 lists content criteria. The real success criteria for me:

- [ ] At least 3 inbound conversations from recruiters/hiring managers explicitly referencing the project
- [ ] At least 1 conversation with a tech lead or engineer who shares the work in their network
- [ ] Project linked from LinkedIn profile and resume by Milestone B
- [ ] At least one Pretext-adjacent person (Cheng Lou, dev-tooling community, layout-engine folks) acknowledges the work
- [ ] Measurable lift in LinkedIn SSI "Engage with insights" component during the publishing window

None of these go in the public PRD. They would mark the project as portfolio bait and discount the methodology in readers' eyes.

## LinkedIn content plan

Six posts, derived from experiments. Each post is short (≤200 words), links to the experiment page, contains one specific surprising finding.

| # | Post angle                                                                          | Tied to            | Target window       |
|---|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------|---------------------|
| 1 | "Pretext is slower than DOM on single-shot small inputs. Here's why that's normal." | Exp 1              | Right after Milestone A |
| 2 | "p50 vs mean: half the perf benchmarks online lie to you. Numbers."                 | Methodology        | +5–7 days           |
| 3 | "300× faster — true or marketing? Per-scenario breakdown."                          | Exp 2              | Around Milestone B  |
| 4 | "Multilingual feed: CJK + Arabic + Korean in one virtualized list. What broke."     | Exp 6              | Phase 3             |
| 5 | "Streaming AI chat without layout shift: three approaches measured."                | Exp 4              | Phase 3             |
| 6 | "How I built a browser-based benchmarking lab. Methodology."                        | Meta               | After Milestone C   |

Cadence: roughly one post every 4–7 days. Series should run for about 5–6 weeks total.

### Post writing rules

- Written **after** numbers are in. No "soon" posts. No teasers.
- No "thrilled to share" / "excited to announce" / motivational phrasing.
- Every post anchored to a specific number with a link.
- One concrete surprising finding per post; don't summarize the whole experiment.
- Reply to every substantive comment within 24h during the publishing window.

## Time budget

Working in evenings, ~2 hours per session.

- Phase 1 (to Milestone A): 10–14 evenings. Realistic target: 2 weeks.
- Phase 2 (to Milestone B): 10–14 more evenings. Realistic target: weeks 3–5.
- Phase 3 (to Milestone C): 10–14 more evenings. Realistic target: weeks 6–8.

If a stage starts blowing past 4 evenings without clear end in sight: stop, write up what's done, move on.
